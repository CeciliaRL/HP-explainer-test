Sebastian was a young individual.


== Etymology ==
Sebastian is a name with Greek and Latin origins meaning "venerable; majestic; revered; exalted."
As a Greek word meaning "venerable one", Sebastos was an honorific used by the ancient Greeks to render the Roman imperial title of Augustus (being an honorific for Roman emperors that was a translation of the Latin Augustus) that formed the basis of a new system of court titles for the Byzantine Empire.
Sebasteia was a military-civilian province of the Byzantine Empire .
