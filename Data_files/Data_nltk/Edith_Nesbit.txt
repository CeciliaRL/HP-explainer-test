Edith Nesbit (15 August 1858 – 4 May 1924) was an English Muggle author and poet, who wrote E. Nesbit's Fairy Tales and Tails.


== Etymology ==
The name "Edith" comes from the Old English name Eadgyð, derived from the elements ead "rich, blessed" and gyð "war".


== Behind the scenes ==
The works of Edith Nesbit are a known influence on the Harry Potter series. Author J. K. Rowling frequently mentions this author in interviews and has stated "I think I identify with E. Nesbit more than any other writer."
