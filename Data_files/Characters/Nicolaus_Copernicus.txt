'''Nicolaus Copernicus''' (b. [[19 February]], [[1473]]—[[24 May]], [[1543]]) was a [[Poland|Polish]] [[Wikipedia:Polymath|polymath]] who lived during the Renaissance era.<ref name="wikipedia">"[http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolaus_Copernicus Nicolaus Copernicus]" at [[Wikipedia:Main Page|Wikipedia]]</ref> He was a contemporary of [[Phillipus von Hohenheim|Paracelsus]] and [[Leonardo Da Vinci]].<ref name="CSpc">{{COS|G}} - PC version</ref>

Copernicus is best known for his [[Astronomy (class)|astronomical]] work. This included coming up with the first comprehensive heliocentric cosmology, which displaced the [[Orrery|Earth]] from the centre of the universe.<ref name="wikipedia" /> In addition, he was a mathematician, astronomer, physician, quadrilingual polyglot, classical scholar, translator, artist, Catholic cleric, jurist, governor, military leader, diplomat and economist.<ref name="wikipedia" />
