'''Jacob Grimm''' ([[1785]]–[[1863]]) and '''Wilhelm Grimm''' ([[1786]]–[[1859]]), commonly referred to collectively as the '''Brothers Grimm''', were [[Germany|German]] philologists and cultural historians who specialised in folklore.<ref name="wikipedia">"[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brothers_Grimm Brothers Grimm] on Wikipedia</ref>

==History==
In [[1812]], they published ''[[wikipedia:Grimms' Fairy Tales|Children's and Household Tales]]'', a collection of German fairy tales they had gathered.<ref name="wikipedia" /> Among the stories it contained were "[[Cinderella]]" and "[[Snow White and the Seven Dwarves]]".<ref name="wikipedia" /> These became favourites of [[Non-magic people|Muggle]] children but remained relatively unknown among [[wizardkind|wizarding]] families.<ref name="dh7">{{DH|7}}</ref> However, the [[Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry|Hogwarts]] [[Hogwarts Library|library]] kept at least one copy of ''[[Household Stories]]'', an English translation of the tales by [[Lucy Crane]].<ref name="hple">{{HPLE|G}} {{See image|File:Household Stories.png}}</ref>
