'''Victoria''' (full name '''Alexandrina Victoria'''; [[24 May]] [[1819]] – [[22 January]] [[1901]]) was a [[Non-magic people|Muggle]] who reigned as [[Queen]] of the United Kingdom of [[Great Britain]] and [[Ireland]] from [[1837]] to [[1901]].<ref name="wikipedia">"[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Victoria Queen Victoria]" on Wikipedia</ref>

[[Evangeline Orpington]], the [[Minister for Magic]] from [[1849]] to [[1855]], was a close friend of Queen Victoria.<ref name="pm-ministers">{{PM|ministers-for-magic}}</ref> The queen never suspected that Orpington was a [[wizardkind|witch]].<ref name="pm-ministers" />

Victoria was buried on [[4 February]] [[1901]].<ref name="wikipedia" /> The Minister for Magic at the time, [[Faris Spavin]], attended her [[Funeral of Victoria|funeral]] in an admiral's hat and spats.<ref name="pm-ministers" /> This was the final act that made the [[Wizengamot]] suggest that Spavin, then 147 years old, retire.<ref name="pm-ministers" />

A [[portrait]] of Victoria hung in the abandoned [[Old Bell Tower|old bell tower]] near the [[Quidditch pitch|pitch]] where the [[Chudley Cannons]] practised.<ref name="fb-cases">{{FBC}} {{see image|File:QueenVictoriaPortrait.jpg}}</ref>

Victoria was a descendant of King [[William I]] and a great-great-grandmother of Queen [[Elizabeth II]].
