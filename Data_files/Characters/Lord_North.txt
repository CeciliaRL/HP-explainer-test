'''Frederick North, 2nd Earl of Guilford''' ([[13 April]], [[1732]] – [[5 August]], [[1792]]), more often known by his courtesy title, '''Lord North''', was the [[Non-magic people|Muggle]] [[Prime Minister (title)|Prime Minister]] from [[28 January]], [[1770]] to [[22 March]], [[1782]].<ref name="Wikipedia">[[Wikipedia:Frederick North, Lord North|"Frederick North, Lord North"]] on [[Wikipedia:Main page|Wikipedia]]</ref>

==Biography==
Lord North called [[Minister for Magic]] [[Porteus Knatchbull]] in confidentially in [[1782]], to see whether he could help with [[George III|King George III]]'s emerging mental instability. Word leaked out that Lord North believed in wizards, and he was forced to resign after a motion of no confidence.<ref name="PMministers">{{WW|ministers-for-magic}}</ref>

==Behind the scenes==
*In reality, Lord North resigned from Prime Minister after a vote of no confidence resulting from the {{wplink|Siege of Yorktown}}, which was the decisive battle against the [[Great Britain|British]] in the {{wplink|American Revolutionary War}}.
**Moreover, he subsequently served as Home Secretary and Leader of the House of Commons (jointly in the latter role with {{wplink|Charles James Fox}}) in a ministry nominally headed by {{wplink|William Cavendish-Bentinck, 3rd Duke of Portland}}.
