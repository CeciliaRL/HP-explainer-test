This '''boy''' was an [[United States of America|American]] [[Non-magic people|No-Maj]] who lived in [[New York]] in the early [[20th century]].<ref name="FBS"/>

==History==
In [[1926]], the boy went to the [[Second Salem Church]], where poor children were fed in exchange for distributing leaflets. While he was waiting in line for soup, [[Unidentified poor boy|another boy]] asked [[New Salem Philanthropic Society]] leader [[Mary Lou Barebone]] if the birthmark on his face was a "[[magic|witch's]] mark," to which she replied that it was not.<ref name="FBS">{{FB|S}}</ref>

==Behind the scenes==
*The boy was portrayed by [[Leo Heller]] in the film ''[[Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)|Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them]]''.
