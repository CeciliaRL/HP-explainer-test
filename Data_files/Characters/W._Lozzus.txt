'''W. Lozzus''' was an arts and culture reporter for ''[[The New York Ghost]]'' in the [[1920s]].<ref name="FB-dec1">{{FB|F}} {{See image|File:NewYorkGhost-1December1927.jpg}}</ref><ref name="nyghost-dec6">{{WWMM|1}} {{see image|File:NewYorkGhost-6December1926.jpg}}</ref>

They wrote an article entitled "Black magic: how to get the most out of your every day robes" that was published in the [[1 December]] [[1926]] issue of the paper.<ref name="FB-dec1" /> Five days later, on [[6 December]], the paper printed a report by them about a planned [[Wizard Broadway]] adaptation of the popular book ''[[Big Foot's Last Stand]]''.<ref name="nyghost-dec6" /> This was followed by a report by them in [[7 December]]'s sunset edition about how casting of the play had begun and was progressing quickly.<ref name="nyghost-dec7">{{CB}} {{see image|File:The New York Ghost - 7 Dec 1926.png}}</ref>
