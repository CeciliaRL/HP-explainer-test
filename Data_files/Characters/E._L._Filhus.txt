'''E. L. Filhus''' was the editor for ''[[The New York Ghost]]'' in the [[1920s]].<ref name="nyghost">{{WWMM|1}} {{see image|File:NewYorkGhost-6December1926.jpg}}</ref><ref name="Featurette">{{FB|F}} {{see image|File:E.L. Filbus.png}}</ref>

==Biography==
In the [[1 December]] [[1926]] issue, Filhus wrote an editorial about the threat [[Gellert Grindelwald]] posed to the [[United States of America|United States]], titled "MACUSA... is the USA ready?".<ref name="Featurette" /><ref name="FB-dec1">{{FB|F}} {{See image|File:NewYorkGhost-1December1927.jpg}}</ref>

An editorial of theirs, entitled "[[Gellert Grindelwald|Grindelwald's]] Stratagem? World Domination?", appeared in the [[6 December]] sunrise edition.<ref name="dec6sunrise">{{FB|F}} {{See image|File:The New York Ghost - 6 Dec 1926 - Sunrise Early Edition.png}}</ref> In the day's sunset edition, Filhus wrote a report about an address that [[Seraphina Picquery]], the [[President of the Magical Congress of the United States of America|President]] of [[Magical Congress of the United States of America|MACUSA]], was slated to give to the "fearful" American [[wizardkind|wizarding]] community.<ref name="nyghost" /> An editorial of theirs on an unknown subject was published in the same issue.<ref name="nyghost" />

They wrote a report, published in the [[7 December]] 1926 sunset edition, about how President Picquery was calling for [[Auror]]s to be on "full alert" because foreign wizarding powers had not yet committed to helping deal with the recent [[International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy|secrecy]] breaches in [[New York]].<ref name="nyghost-dec7">{{CB}} {{see image|File:The New York Ghost - 7 Dec 1926.png}}</ref> This issue also featured an editorial by them on an unknown subject.<ref name="nyghost-dec7" />

==Behind the scenes==
*This character may have been named after [[Eduardo Lima]], a graphic artist who worked on every [[Harry Potter films|''Harry Potter'' film]] from ''[[Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (film)|Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban]]'' onward.
