==Etymology==
"{{w|Singh}}" is a common Indian surname, derived from the Sanskrit ''sinha'' meaning "lion." The name Singh is mainly used by followers of the {{w|Sikhs|Sikh}} religion from Punjab, where it is mandatory for all baptised males, per instruction from Guru Gobind Singh.
