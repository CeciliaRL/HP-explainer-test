}}'''Shikoba Wolfe''' was an [[United States of America|American]] [[wizardkind|wizard or witch]].<ref name="pottermore-1920s">{{WW|1920s-wizarding-america-en}}</ref> Wolfe, along with [[Johannes Jonker]], [[Thiago Quintana]], and [[Violetta Beauvais]], was one of the four [[wandmaker]]s who served [[North America]] during the early [[20th century]].<ref name="pottermore-1920s" />

==Biography==
Of [[Choctaw]] descent, Wolfe was best known for crafting [[wand]]s that were intricately carved. Wands made by Wolfe contained [[Thunderbird]] [[Thunderbird tail feather|tail feathers]] as [[Wand core|cores]] and were generally believed to be extremely powerful, but difficult to master. They were especially sought after by practitioners of [[Transfiguration]].<ref name="pottermore-1920s" />

==History==
Shikoba Wolfe was mentioned on a Shikoba Wolfe Fine Wands advert in [[The New York Ghost]] in [[1926]].<ref name="FBF">{{see image|File:New York Ghost - 2 December 1926.jpg}}</ref>

==Behind the scenes==
*This individual's gender is left unspecified on {{PM}}. Various official translations vary the gender. In its native language of Choctaw it is gender neutral, however historically its more often used as a female name.
