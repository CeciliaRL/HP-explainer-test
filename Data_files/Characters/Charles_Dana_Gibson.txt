'''Charles Dana Gibson''' ([[14 September]] [[1867]] – [[23 December]] [[1944]]) was an [[United States of America|American]] [[Non-magic people|Muggle]] illustrator known for his creation of the "[[wikipedia:Gibson Girl|Gibson Girl]]".<ref name="wiki1">"[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Dana_Gibson Charles Dana Gibson]" on Wikipedia</ref>

==Biography==
Former [[France|French]] prime minister [[Georges Clemenceau]] came to the United States to deliver a talk on [[6 December]] [[1926]].<ref name="fb-paper">"[http://www.gazette-du-sorcier.com/Compte-rendu-ouverture-de-l-exposition-Harry-Potter-a-Bruxelles-pour-Noel Compte-rendu : ouverture de l’exposition Harry Potter à Bruxelles pour Noël]" from the Gazette du Sorcier {{see image|File:New York Chronicle - Monday Dec 1926 (HQ).png}}</ref><ref name="eveningworld">[http://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/lccn/sn83030193/1922-11-21/ed-1/seq-1/ Front page of the 21 November 1922 issue of ''The Evening World''] from Chronicling America</ref> He hid himself away on the upper floor of  Gibson's home on [[East 73rd Street]] in [[Manhattan]] to prepare his speech for that evening.<ref name="fb-paper" /><ref name="eveningworld" /> Many admirers flocked to the house, hoping to secure interviews or autographs, but only a reporter for the ''[[New York Chronicle]]'' was granted access.<ref name="fb-paper" /><ref name="eveningworld" /> 

==Behind the scenes==
*The text of the article mentioning [[wikipedia:Charles Dana Gibson|Charles Dana Gibson]] in the [[:File:New York Chronicle - Monday Dec 1926 (HQ).png|prop issue]] of the ''[[New York Chronicle]]'' featured in ''[[Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them (film)|Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them]]'' was taken from the real [[21 November]] [[1922]] issue of ''[[wikipedia:The Evening World|The Evening World]]''.<ref name="eveningworld" /> This represents an anachronism, as the film is set in [[1926]], but the actual historical event referenced happened in 1922.
